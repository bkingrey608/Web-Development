The Early Years

I was born June 8, 1971, in Columbus, GA, to middle-class parents who worked hard to provide myself and my younger brother Jarrod the best a kid could ever need. My dad was a Civil Engineer who traveled weekly for Southern Railroad. We were heavily into sports, and my parents and extended family were always there to support us. My mother and her sister both worked in Civil Service at Ft. Benning. My grandfather retired as a US Army Sgt and finished his working career in Civil Service at Ft. Benning as well. My other grandfather was a decorated police officer who served for over 35 years as a Lieutenant for the Columbus Police Dept. Many ask if I am a “military brat,” and my answer is always “no.”

As a student, I excelled from Elementary school through High School as a member of the National Honor Society. I played baseball and football for Columbus High School, and even though I received scholarship offers to play baseball in college, I knew I’d never make a career in the sport. As an engineer, my dad played a big part in my choice of Georgia Tech to pursue an engineering degree. My college years were very difficult. Studying Mechanical Engineering, working part-time, and competing nationally in Racquetball consumed all my time.

Career

After six years of college, I got a break. I got a job as a Field Service Engineer for a German-based Electronics/Software company. I spent nine years traveling the country full-time installing Printing Presses. I had no background in electronics, other than a car stereo hobby, and learned all my skills on the job. I’d say it took about two years to become an expert on the Hardware side – Power Distribution, Relay logic, VFDs, Pneumatics, etc. During that time, I married and had two children, Jacob and Hudson. They are now 23 and 17 years old, respectively! For the last 15 years I’ve focused mostly on the Software side – PLCs, HMIs, SCADA, etc. and I no longer must travel full-time. I work as a Controls Support Engineer for Vanderlande, a Netherlands-based company that is an OEM for sorting equipment for numerous international customers such as airports, warehouses, Amazon, and Walmart.

Return to School

Not long after I began working at Vanderlande, I saw a shift in the industry away from Low-Level controls (PLCs), and toward High-Level controls (PC-Based). I found myself having to reach out to software engineers far too often and decided to do something about that. Personally, I remarried and my wife, Crystal helped motivate me to return to school. I’m now pursuing a bachelor’s degree in IT at Kennesaw State University to learn new skills that will benefit my career. After two years at KSU now, much of what I’m studying I’m very familiar with due to my experience. I thoroughly enjoyed the two Java classes I’ve taken and currently I’m studying Web Development which is completely new to me. It’s already making a difference in my career. I’m much more comfortable and skilled at studying code written in high-level languages and look forward to learning more.
